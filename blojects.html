Projects

Visual illusion

While I was at Yotsumoto lab at the University of Tokyo, I programmed several novel visual illusions as a programming practice for psychophysics experiments. Some of the works were chosen for the illusion contest hosted by the Japanese psychonomic society. Unlike other prize-winning works, mine so far haven't gone viral, unfortunately.

Electronics

I have recently taken up some simple electronics projects as a hobby, hoping to acquire some skills useful at the lab. I'm also planning to build some simple devices that mimic some interesting neural circuits found in flies.

Compositions

I used to play the saxophone and clarinet in bands and orchestras up until few years ago. I also have been writing some classical-styled music since when I was a high school junior, mostly for the piano or small ensembles. I might make a list of and upload sheet music of those pieces at some point.
